To sell on Amazon, you need to create a Seller Central account and choose between an Individual or Professional selling plan. From there, you list your products, set prices, and decide how to handle fulfillment — either shipping orders yourself (FBM) or using Fulfillment by Amazon (FBA), where Amazon stores, packs, and ships on your behalf. 🔗
Steps to Start Selling on Amazon
Create a Seller Account: Go to sell.amazon.com and register. You'll need a business email, bank account, government ID, tax information, and a credit card.
Choose a Selling Plan: The Individual plan costs $0.99 per item sold (best for under 40 sales/month). The Professional plan is $39.99/month flat with access to advanced tools, ads, and Buy Box eligibility.
List Your Products: Add items via Seller Central using an existing ASIN or create a new listing. Include title, images, bullet points, description, and keywords.
Select a Fulfillment Method: With FBA, Amazon handles storage, shipping, and customer service. With FBM, you ship directly to buyers and manage returns yourself.
Key Costs & Fees
Amazon charges a referral fee (typically 8–15% of the sale price depending on category), plus FBA fees if applicable. Additional costs may include storage fees, advertising spend, and returns processing. Use the Amazon Revenue Calculator to estimate your margins before listing.
