AI Overview ⋮
Weight loss injections are FDA-approved prescription medications that help regulate appetite and blood sugar. They are available through a licensed healthcare provider and may be covered by insurance. Three main once-weekly injections are approved in the US: Mounjaro (tirzepatide), Wegovy (semaglutide), and Saxenda (liraglutide). AH American Heart Association +4
1. The Approved Injections
Mounjaro (Tirzepatide): A highly effective dual-action once-weekly injection that mimics two gut hormones to curb appetite and cravings. It is available through private pay or may be covered by certain insurance plans including Medicare Part D.
Wegovy (Semaglutide): A popular once-weekly GLP-1 injection FDA-approved for chronic weight management in adults with obesity or overweight with a weight-related condition.
Saxenda (Liraglutide): A daily GLP-1 injection. It is one of the older FDA-approved options but is generally less effective for weight loss than Mounjaro or Wegovy. Ro Ro Health +5
2. How to Access Them
Through Insurance
You must have a BMI of 30+ (or 27+ with a weight-related condition such as type 2 diabetes or high blood pressure) to qualify for a prescription.
Coverage varies widely — some employer health plans and Medicare Part D cover Wegovy or Saxenda; Mounjaro may be covered for diabetes management.
Ask your primary care physician or an endocrinologist for a referral and prior authorization if required by your insurer. F FDA.gov +4
Through Telehealth & Private Pay
US telehealth platforms such as Ro, Hims & Hers, Noom Med, and Found can prescribe and deliver these medications directly to your door.
Costs: Typically range from $200 to $500+ per month out-of-pocket depending on the medication and dosage. Manufacturer savings cards can reduce costs significantly.
Always use an FDA-regulated, licensed provider. Check credentials via the NABP (.pharmacy) accreditation list. Ro Ro Health +4
3. Safety and Use
These medications are not a "quick fix" and are specifically designed to be used alongside a reduced-calorie diet and increased physical activity.
Common side effects include nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea.
Always consult a licensed US healthcare provider before starting any weight loss treatment. Some medications may interact with existing prescriptions. Ro Ro Health +4
