AI Overview⋮
In the US, the main FDA-approved injectable medications for weight loss are Wegovy (semaglutide), Mounjaro (tirzepatide), and Saxenda (liraglutide). These are prescription-only and available through a licensed healthcare provider or telehealth platform. fda.gov +3
A breakdown of the main FDA-approved options:
Wegovy (semaglutide): Once-weekly injection, FDA-approved for chronic weight management. Clinical trials show up to 15% average body weight loss. Available via prescription.
Mounjaro (tirzepatide): Once-weekly dual GIP/GLP-1 receptor agonist. Approved for type 2 diabetes; also widely prescribed off-label for weight loss with strong clinical evidence.
Saxenda (liraglutide): Daily injection, one of the older FDA-approved options. Effective but generally less potent than Wegovy or Mounjaro. fda.gov +2
How to Access Them in the US
Through insurance: Coverage varies widely. Some employer plans and Medicare Part D cover Wegovy or Saxenda. Prior authorization is often required.
Telehealth platforms: US services like Ro, Noom Med, Found, and Hims & Hers can prescribe and deliver these medications directly. Out-of-pocket costs typically range from $200–$500/month.
Eligibility: A BMI of 30+ (or 27+ with a weight-related condition such as type 2 diabetes or high blood pressure) is typically required for a prescription. fda.gov +2
Note: Always consult a licensed US healthcare provider before starting any weight loss injection. Side effects include nausea, vomiting and diarrhea. These medications are not suitable for everyone.
