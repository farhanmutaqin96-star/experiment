AI Overview ⋮
Measuring for curtains is simple: measure the width of your track or pole (not the window), and the drop from the top of the pole to your desired length. Always use a metal tape measure, as cloth tapes can stretch and cause inaccuracies. Y YouTube · Spotlight Stores +3
Follow these simple steps for a perfect fit:
1. Measure the Width
Curtain Poles: Measure the width of the pole between the two decorative ends (finials).
Curtain Tracks: Measure the full length of the track from end to end.
Rule for Fullness: The combined width of your curtains should be at least double your pole or track width to achieve a natural, gathered look when closed. D Dunelm.com +1
2. Measure the Drop (Length)
First, decide where you want your curtains to finish, then measure from the attachment point on your pole or track:
To the Sill: Measure to 1 cm above the windowsill.
Below the Sill: Measure 15 to 20 cm below the sill to block out more light and keep them clear of any radiators.
To the Floor: Measure to 1 cm above the floor so they hang straight and avoid dragging. P The Pure Edit +2
3. Factor in the Heading Type
The header style affects where you start measuring from for your length: Next
Eyelet Curtains: Measure from the top of the pole. (Note: The pole itself should sit about 15 cm above the window recess.)
Pencil Pleat or Pinch Pleat: Measure from the underside of the curtain ring or track glider. Y YouTube · Dunelm +2
For more specific guidance, visual examples, or to calculate your exact sizing, check the sources in the panel or use an online curtain size calculator.
